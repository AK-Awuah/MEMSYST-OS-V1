MemSyst Architecture Directive
Technology Independence, Cost Optimization, Scalability & Maintainability
This directive supplements the existing Memsyst Platform Architecture and must be treated as a core architectural requirement for all current and future development.

Objective
Design and implement MemSyst as a platform that uses Firebase as an infrastructure service rather than being tightly coupled to Firebase-specific implementations.
The architecture must prioritize:
* Long-term maintainability 
* Cost efficiency 
* Security 
* High performance 
* Scalability 
* Vendor flexibility 
* Ease of future migration 
* Multi-tenant SaaS operation 
* Low operational overhead 
The platform must remain capable of evolving beyond Firebase in the future without requiring major application rewrites.

Core Architectural Principle
Technology Independence Principle
Business functionality must never depend directly on Firebase SDKs, Firestore structures, Cloud Functions, or any vendor-specific implementation.
All business modules must communicate through defined service and repository layers.
The application must be architected so that Firebase can be replaced in the future with alternative technologies (PostgreSQL, Supabase, AWS, Azure, etc.) with minimal impact on business logic and user-facing functionality.

Required Architecture Layers

* UI components must never call Firebase directly. 
* UI components must never contain business logic. 
* Business workflows must never be implemented inside pages or components. 
* Business logic must never depend on Firebase SDKs. 
* Repository interfaces must shield the application from infrastructure implementation details.

MemSyst Financial Infrastructure & Revenue Model Directive
Purpose
This directive defines the financial architecture, revenue model, fee management framework, and sustainability requirements for the MemSyst Platform.
The objective is to ensure that MemSyst operates as a financially sustainable multi-tenant SaaS platform while providing organizations with flexible fee configuration and revenue generation capabilities.
This directive must be incorporated into the Financial Infrastructure Module (Phase 2 ñ Stage 5) and treated as a core platform requirement.

Financial Architecture Principles
The financial system must support:
* Platform sustainability
* Multi-tenant operations
* Configurable organizational fees
* Revenue sharing
* Platform commissions
* Transaction processing
* Wallet management
* Auditability
* Financial reporting
* Future payment gateway integrations
All financial operations must be fully auditable and traceable.
No financial transaction may be deleted.
Financial records must support complete historical tracking.

Revenue Model Structure
The MemSyst revenue model shall consist of four revenue layers:
Layer 1 ñ Platform Subscription Revenue
This is the primary recurring revenue source.
Organizations may be assigned subscription plans such as:
Starter
* Monthly Fee
* Quarterly Fee
* Annual Fee
Growth
* Monthly Fee
* Quarterly Fee
* Annual Fee
Enterprise
* Custom Pricing
The platform must support:
* Plan creation
* Plan assignment
* Plan upgrades
* Plan downgrades
* Plan renewals
* Trial periods
* Grace periods
* Subscription suspension
Subscription plans must be configurable by Super Admin.

Layer 2 ñ Platform Commission Revenue
The platform shall support configurable commissions on selected transactions.
Initial supported categories:
Accounts
* Account Registration
* Account Renewal
* Account Transfer
* Account Upgrade
Print Services
* QR Code ID Card Printing
* QR Code Certificate Printing
Content & Listings
* Marketplace Posts
* Training Posts
* Other Listing Categories
* Sponsored/Advertising Posts
Wallet Transactions
* Wallet Funding Processing
* Wallet Withdrawals
The default configuration shall support percentage-based commissions.
Example:
Account Registration       10%

Wallet Funding             5%

All percentages must be configurable.
No values should be hardcoded.

Layer 3 ñ Communication Revenue Recovery
Communication services must support cost recovery and optional markup.
Supported channels:
* Email
* SMS
* Push Notifications
* In-App Notifications
* OTP Services
The platform must support:

Actual provider cost.

SMS Charge:        0.50
Markup:            0.20

* Communication costs
* Communication revenue
* Communication profit
Note:
Email, Push, and In-App notifications should primarily support cost recovery and operational management rather than act as primary profit centers.

Advertising must operate as a dedicated revenue stream.
Supported advertising products may include:
